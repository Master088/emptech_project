5 Contemporary Art you need to see

Contemporary art is the art of today, produced by artists who are living in the twenty-first century. As such, it reflects the complex issues that shape our diverse, global, and rapidly changing world. Through their work, many contemporary artists explore personal or cultural identity, offer critiques of social and institutional structure or even attempt to redefine art itself.

Today we’re breaking down 5 key contemporary artworks that you need to know to fully understand this artistic period!

Title: Planting Rice (1962)

Artist: Fernando Amorsolo (Filipino, 1892-1972)

Medium: Oil on canvas Size; 71 x 97 cm. (28 x 38.2 in.)

Background about the Artist and his Art

Fernando Amorsolo, a Filipino painter (1892-1972) was dominant in creating visual arts for decades before and after the second world war. He was popular for his artworks that was characterized by the scene of Filipino countryside, harmoniously composed and richly coloured, saturated with bright sunlight and populated by beautiful happy people. Amorsolo was committed to his two fundamental ideas in his arts which are; classical notion of idealism wherein truth was found through harmony, balance, and beauty. His second fundamental idea is the conservative concept of Filipino characters rooted in rural communities, and the cycle of village life. His artworks usually depicts people and barrio scenes in the Philippines.

Planting Rice (1962) Represents the significance to our culture, it was a representational art that portrays the life of the Filipino farmers in 1962. The painting was engaged in every day life of Filipino farmers and their activities. The painting captures the traditional Filipino occupation of men and women in a provincial setting for the workers beside the mountains which farmers wears the traditional clothes for farming in the year 1950s. Painting “Planting Rice” shows the underlying values of hardwork, endurance, and the lifestyle of Filipino farmers.

Title:Autumn Rhythm (1950)

Artist:Jackson Pollock

Arts Style:Dip Painting (Abstract Art)

Background about the Artist and his Art

Created at the peak of Jackson Pollock’s career, Autumn Rhythm is a perfect example of the drip-painting technique. The technique, which is specific to Pollock, made him famous due to its originality. To create his works, Pollock stood on his canvas and dripped diluted paint onto the canvas, letting it fall naturally. He would drip, splash, scrape and pour paint onto his canvas. For the artist, the movement and paints themselves drove his art. This unique method of painting is considered to be the artist’s trademark. Ashley Ashley Eman Areola

Title: Untitled (1982)

Artist: Keith Haring

Arts Style:Pop Art

Background about the Artist and his Art

This painting sums up the Pop Art style of Keith Haring, with dynamic figures and a simple composition. From his beginnings as a graffiti artist on the New York subway, Keith Haring began his career with his immediately recognizable figures and patterns. One of his most commonly represented symbols is the heart. He used his work to popularize important messages about sexuality and AIDS during a time when the stigma and taboo surrounding these topics were still prevalent.

Title:The Girl Who Had Everything (1998)

Artist:Cecily Brown

Arts Style: Abstract Painting

Background about the artist and her art

British artist Cecily Brown is known for her luxurious and extremely textured contemporary artworks. Her art is a mix between that of the baroque masters and abstract expressionism. She mixes color and texture with sexually suggestive figures and biomorphic forms that stand out against the abstract swirls that dominate her paintings. By simply alluding to forms rather than offering a concrete representation, the artist forces the viewer to examine the paintings in depth, from which the erotic images come into focus.
